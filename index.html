Since 1995, Maryland has stood firm on its “life means life” absolutism - a phrase then-governor Parris Glendening has come to regret. No inmate serving a life sentence for crimes committed as a minor has been released under Maryland’s parole system.

Maryland is one of only three states that requires the governor's signature to parole inmates sentenced to life. Democratic and Republican governors alike have rejected any parole recommendations for juvenile lifers. It’s been more than two decades and over 300 parole-eligible juveniles lifers remain incarcerated in Maryland.
