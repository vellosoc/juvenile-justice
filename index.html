Growing Up Behind Bars

The United States is the only country in the world that condemns people to life in prison for crimes committed under the age of 18. Yet in the “lock ‘em up” climate of the 1990s, thousands of juveniles were given life sentences without parole.



     


The U.S. Supreme Court has since ruled this unconstitutional. It is estimated more than 2,000 are imprisoned in what the Court says is cruel and unusual punishment. The state of Maryland alone has 312 such juveniles. Almost twenty-five years passed without a singe parole.
