Meet The Juvenile Lifers

Earl Young, Mary Brown-Bey and Calvin McNeill spent nearly 100 years incarcerated collectively. Here are their stories.




EARL YOUNG

By DELON THORNTON


Earl Young spent 34 years and 7 months incarcerated. Arrested at 16 in 1984 with no prior record, Young was convicted of murder, armed robbery and illegal possession of a firearm. A year later he was sentenced to concurrent sentences of the balance of his natural life for murder, ten years for the armed robbery and 3 years for posession of a weapon.
